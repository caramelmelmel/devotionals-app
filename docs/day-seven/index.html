Day 7 Devotional

Scripture​

1 Corinthians 15:51-58 (NLT)​

51 But let me reveal to you a wonderful secret. We will not all die, but we will all be transformed!52 It will happen in a moment, in the blink of an eye, when the last trumpet is blown. For when the trumpet sounds, those who have died will be raised to live forever. And we who are living will also be transformed. 53 For our dying bodies must be transformed into bodies that will never die; our mortal bodies must be transformed into immortal bodies.54 Then, when our dying bodies have been transformed into bodies that will never die, this Scripture will be fulfilled:

“Death is swallowed up in victory.

55 O death, where is your victory? O death, where is your sting?”56 For sin is the sting that results in death, and the law gives sin its power. 57 But thank God! He gives us victory over sin and death through our Lord Jesus Christ.58 So, my dear brothers and sisters, be strong and immovable. Always work enthusiastically for the Lord, for you know that nothing you do for the Lord is ever useless.

Copy and Paste Version​

1 Corinthians 15:51-58 (NLT)
But let me reveal to you a wonderful secret. We will not all die, but we will all
be transformed! It will happen in a moment, in the blink of an eye, when the
last trumpet is blown. For when the trumpet sounds, those who have died will
be raised to live forever. And we who are living will also be transformed. For
our dying bodies must be transformed into bodies that will never die; our mortal
bodies must be transformed into immortal bodies.

Then, when our dying bodies have been transformed into bodies that will never
die, this Scripture will be fulfilled:

  “Death is swallowed up in victory.
   O death, where is your victory?
  O death, where is your sting?”

For sin is the sting that results in death, and the law gives sin its power. But
thank God! He gives us victory over sin and death through our Lord Jesus Christ.
So, my dear brothers and sisters, be strong and immovable. Always work
enthusiastically for the Lord, for you know that nothing you do for the Lord
is ever useless.

A copy of the formatted bible verse to remind you to share with your friend.

Devotion​

Two of our biggest enemies have to be sin and death.

Sin leads to death. Death is the penalty of our sin but Jesus has given us victory over death and there won’t be any ‘come back’. It is finished and there will be no sequel.

When we see the gruesome death of Jesus on the cross, it shows us how terrible sin is. When Jesus took the penalty for our sin in His body, it was a miraculous day of victory. People would never have imagined that this apparent defeat would actually be the road to the ultimate victory. He did it so sin and death would never again have power over us so that we could walk in overwhelming victory.

I pray that as you read and think about this scripture today, you would have the full understanding of the powerful work of Jesus when He took our sin upon Himself. He set us free and free indeed! Surely an understanding of what Jesus has done for us by defeating sin and death will help us to be strong and immovable. Surely it will motivate us to work enthusiastically for the Lord realising that what we do for Jesus is always helpful and productive.

Just like going to the cross seemed the opposite of a victorious move, what we do for Jesus can feel like it’s not having an impact. But it does. We are sowing seeds and these seeds will always produce a harvest. As we sow our time, finances, gifts, encouragement or even something as simple as a smile, we are doing something great for God and His Kingdom, and none of it is a waste.

Prayer​

Father,

Thank you for going to the cross to defeat sin and death for me. You have provided the way for me to walk in complete victory and today I ask You to empower me to work enthusiastically for You, to be strong and immovable and to see Your Kingdom come on earth as it is in Heaven, through me.

I pray all this in Jesus' name, Amen.
